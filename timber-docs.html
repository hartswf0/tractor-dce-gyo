TIMBER STUDIO
Meta-studio shell. Hosts Courage, WERE, Master, and Symbiogene in one chrome. Owns the shared bus for GOLD snapshots and selection, and decides which instruments are visible (TABS vs 3-UP).
WAG COURAGE
Authoring + GOLD source. Edits MPD text, compiles scenes, and emits GOLD snapshots (mpd_content, stud_skeleton, part_skeletons, ground_violations).
WAG WERE
Skeleton inspector. Turns GOLD studs into per-part/per-layer bones and ORBS, keyed by groupKey = lineNum[:layer]. Can export edited MPD back to Courage.
WAG MASTER
Constraint & room viewer. Places the same GOLD scene into a "containment room" with walls/floor, bones, part dots, and collision overlays driven by GOLD.
SYMBIOGENE
Skeleton lab. Shows raw GOLD structures (studs, part dots, violations) in a neutral grid. Acts as the ontology probe and transform debugger for the other views.

Ontology underneath â€“ what all tools agree on
MPD lines
Type-1 part lines with lineNum, color, position, rotation, and subfile. Courage edits these; WERE + Master map GOLD studs back to them via 1-based lineNum.
groupKey
String key for a part + layer ("12" or "12:0"). WERE uses this to group studs and bones; TIMBER passes it through in selection messages.
stud_skeleton
Per-stud sample points in world space, often annotated with lineNum, layer, color, and problem/ghost flags. Drives bones, ORBS, and cluster analysis.
part_skeletons
Per-part anchors (one per MPD line). Master uses these as control dots; Symbiogene can use them to show handles and anchors across tools.
ground_violations
Per-line violation records. Master tints bones and walls from this; WERE and Symbiogene can surface the same lines as red/orange overlays.
ðŸ§¬ TIMBER does not define new data structures; it insists that all tools speak the same dialect (MPD lines, groupKeys, GOLD skeleton arrays) and wires them together without translation drift.
